I’m Kymberley Apiro — a UK-based multimedia journalist and visual storyteller working across photo and video journalism. My practice sits at the intersection of documentary, breaking news and cultural reporting, shaped by a strong visual instinct and a commitment to editorial integrity.

I’ve worked in fast-paced newsroom environments and on the ground, covering high-profile events, grassroots movements and nuanced human stories. From red carpets to rallies, I approach every assignment with curiosity, precision and respect for context. My work is people-led — focused on the motivations, tensions and environments that shape lived experience.

With a background in film and documentary photography from the University of the Arts London, I bring a cinematic eye to factual storytelling. I’m comfortable shooting, producing and editing under pressure, adapting quickly to evolving situations while maintaining clarity of narrative and tone. I understand what editors need — accuracy, strong visuals, clean audio and efficient delivery.

I’m currently available for freelance photo and video journalism assignments, including breaking news coverage, features, cultural events and long-form documentary work. If you’re looking for a thoughtful, reliable visual journalist who can move fluidly between photography, video and research-led storytelling, I’d love to connect.
